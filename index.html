Ethics Website

Bias

A systematic skew in data or model behavior that leads to unfair outcomes for certain groups.

If an AI hiring tool favors men over women for tech jobs, who is responsible for fixing it? The developers, the company, or society?

Bias

Fairness

The principle that models produce equitable outcomes across different individuals or groups.

Can an AI model ever be truly fair, or will it always reflect the biases of its developers and its training data?

Fairness

Accountability

The assignment of responsibility for the decisions and impacts of NLP systems.

If an AI model makes a harmful decision, who should be held responsible?

Accountability

Transparency

The degree to which data, model behavior, and decision processes are open and understandable.

If an AI model makes a decision that affects your life, should you have the right to know how it made that decision?

Transparency

Explainability

The ability to provide human-understandable reasons for a model’s outputs.

How much should we trust the decision of an AI model if we can't fully understand how it reaches that conclusion?

Explainability

Interpretability

The extent to which a model’s internal mechanisms can be directly understood by humans.

Is it acceptable for an AI model to be a "black box" if it works well, or should we always know how it works?

Interpretability

Alignment

The process of ensuring model behavior conforms to human values and intended goals.

Whose values should ann AI model align with? The values of its developers? The values of its users? The values of society as a whole? What if these conflict with each other?

Alignment

Governance

The frameworks, policies, and processes guiding responsible AI development and deployment.

Should governments regulate AI models, or should developers be allowed to self-regulate? What are the risks of either approach

Governance

Compliance

Adherence to legal, regulatory, and ethical standards.

If an AI model breaks a law, but its developers did not intend for this to happen, can they be held accountable? If not, who (if anybody) can?

Compliance

Trust

User confidence in a model’s reliability, transparency, and behavior.

What does it take for you to trust an AI model?

Trust

Confidentiality

The restriction of data access to authorized entities only.

Should AI models in healthcare have access to sensitive patient data?

Confidentiality

Stewardship

The responsible management of data throughout its lifecycle.

Who should ultimately be held accountable for ensuring responsible data stewardship within an organization?

Stewardship

Retention

Policies governing how long data is stored before deletion.

How long should companies be allowed to keep your data?

Retention

Minimization

The practice of collecting only the data necessary for a specific purpose.

Does strict data minimization hinder the development of more accurate systems?

Minimization

Purpose Limitation

The use of data strictly for the originally specified uses.

If a company uses your data for a different purpose than originally stated that benefits you, is that ethically right?

Purpose Limitation

Consent

User permission for collecting, processing, or using their data in NLP systems.

Is it ethical for companies to use your data if it is publicly available (e.g. via social media), even if you didn't explictly agree to its use?

Consent

Privacy

The protection of personal or sensitive information from unauthorized access or exposure.

To what extent are you willing to trade privacy for convenience or personalized services?

Privacy

Provenance

The origin, history, and transformations of data.

Should companies be required to disclose where their training data comes from?

Provenance

Leakage

The unintended exposure of sensitive or training data during training or inference.

How can leakage affect perceived model performance and what misconceptions could this introduce?

Leakage

Data contamination

The inclusion of evaluation or benchmark data in training data, inflating performance estimates.

How does data contamination affect the credibility of benchmarks and reported performance?

Data contamination

Harm

Negative impacts caused by model behavior, including discrimination, misinformation, or privacy violations.

How could an AI model potentially harm you personally?

Harm

Safety

The implementation of mechanisms that prevent harmful or unintended model behavior.

Should AI companies be required to actively notify its users about the risks of using their model?

Safety

Toxicity

The presence of offensive, abusive, or harmful language in text data or outputs.

Who decides what is considered toxic or not, and how does this differ per medium?

Toxicity

Moderation

The filtering or control of content to enforce safety or policy constraints.

How can moderation be performed consistently, while avoiding possible individual biases of the moderators?

Moderation

Adversarial inputs

Inputs intentionally crafted to exploit model weaknesses or cause errors.

Is it realistic to expect AI models to be fully resistant to adversarial attacks, or should we instead focus on mitigation and response?

Adversarial inputs

Filtering

The removal of undesirable or harmful content from data or outputs.

Who should decide what is undesirable or harmful content?

Filtering

Security

The protection of systems and data from unauthorized access, attacks, or breaches.

How much security is “enough”?

Security

Surveillance

The large-scale monitoring of individuals through data collection and analysis.

Is surveillance ever right?

Surveillance

Manipulation

The use of language models to influence user beliefs or behavior without informed consent.

Should an AI model manipulate someone if its in their best interest?

Manipulation

Misuse

The unintended or inappropriate use of NLP systems.

How can we prevent misuse of AI models?

Misuse

Abuse

The deliberate use of NLP systems to cause harm or deceive.

Where should the line be drawn between misuse and deliberate abuse of AI systems?

Abuse

Generalization

The ability of a model to perform well on unseen data outside its training set.

What could cause an AI model to work perfectly in a lab but fail in the real world?

Generalization

Calibration

The alignment between predicted probabilities and actual outcomes.

How could the calibration of a model be improved?

Calibration

Uncertainty

The degree of confidence associated with a model’s predictions.

How certain do you think an AI model should be of an answer before providing it? It is good enough to provide any answer, even if it's very uncertain?

Uncertainty

Benchmark

A standardized dataset or metric used to compare model performance.

Benchmark

Evaluation

The measurement of model performance using technical and ethical metrics.

How can evaluation of a model influence its impact on the world?

Evaluation

The generation of fluent but factually incorrect or unsupported content.

Red Teaming

Monitoring

The continuous observation of model behavior in deployed environments.

Monitoring

Feedback

Feedback

Human in the Loop

Tradeoffs

Standardization

The establishment of common practices, formats, or benchmarks.

Standardization

Interoperability

The ability of different systems to exchange and use data

Interoperability

Inference

The "doing" part of AI, where a trained model applies its learned knowledge to new data, producing outputs like predictions, interpretations, or generated responses

Inference

The presence and portrayal of different demographic or social groups in data.
